Lesson: Past Tense vs Past Participle

Past tense tells what happened in the past. Past participle is a verb form often used with have/has/had (perfect tenses) or in the passive voice.

1) Past Tense (Simple Past)

Use: action finished in the past.
Often with: yesterday, last week, in 2020, ago.
I walked to school.
She ate an apple.
They went home.

2) Past Participle

Use: with have/has/had OR in passive voice.
Examples: has eaten, had gone, was broken.
I have walked to school.
She has eaten an apple.
The window was broken.

Regular verbs (easy rule)

For many regular verbs, past tense and past participle look the same: -ed.

play → played (past) / played (past participle)
clean → cleaned (past) / cleaned (past participle)

Irregular verbs (must practise)

Some verbs change differently. You must learn them.

go → went (past) / gone (past participle)
eat → ate (past) / eaten (past participle)
write → wrote (past) / written (past participle)
Super Summary:
• Past tense = finished action (I went).
• Past participle = used with have/has/had or passive (I have gone / It was broken).
